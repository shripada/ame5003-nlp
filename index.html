MSIS Manipal · AME 5003 & AME 5053

Principles of Natural Language Processing

Everything for this course — lessons, labs and the glossary — lives here.

Start here

Labs run in Google Colab. You need a Google account and a browser. Nothing to install.

Open Lab 1, then immediately do File → Save a copy in Drive. Work in your own copy, or you will lose it when the tab closes.

Lessons

Session 01 What NLP is, and your first tool Why language is hard for computers, and regular expressions. Session 02 Capturing, choosing, and the greedy trap Groups, alternation, word boundaries, and the bug everybody writes once. Session 03 Tokenization Splitting text into words, and why it turns out to be hard. Session 04 Cleaning up text: normalization and stop words Deciding which differences in text do not matter — and why the obvious cleanup is sometimes the mistake. Session 05 Stemming and lemmatization Cutting words to a root — the fast crude way and the slow correct way, and which one your task needs. Session 06 Part-of-speech tagging Labelling each word with its grammatical job — and why the same word needs a different label in different places.

Labs

Lab 01 · opens in Colab Refresher and setup Get Colab working, and refresh the Python this course leans on. Lab 02 · opens in Colab Information extraction with regular expressions Pull amounts, dates and accounts out of messy bank SMS — and find where regex stops working. Lab 03 · opens in Colab Cleaning text: tokenize, normalize, stem, remove stop words The standard preprocessing steps — and when each one quietly destroys your meaning.

The books

All prescribed. The first two are free online.

  • Speech and Language Processing — Jurafsky & Martin. The main text. Free.
  • Natural Language Processing with Python — Bird, Klein & Loper. Free.
  • Practical Natural Language Processing — Vajjala et al. Code is free.
  • Natural Language Processing with PyTorch — Rao & McMahan. Code is free.
  • A Primer on Neural Network Models for NLP — Goldberg. Free.
